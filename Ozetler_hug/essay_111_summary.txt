Asthma has become one of the most common diseases in the United States and the world. 25 million people nationwide have asthma. The most common risk factors include a genetic predisposition to asthma, conditions related to allergic reactions, having serious respiratory infections as a child, and being exposed to various irritants and chemicals at work.
