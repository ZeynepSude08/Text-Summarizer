Heart failure (HF) is one of the leading causes of hospitalization among older adults. There are approximately 6 million Americans with the diagnosed varieties of HF, who require continual medical services. This paper examines the two types of HF – systolic and diastolic – and explains the differences between the varieties based on the case study.
