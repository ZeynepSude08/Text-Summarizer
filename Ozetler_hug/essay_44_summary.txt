Good Country People is a short allegorical story about the dual nature of religious workers, loneliness, and egocentrism. Through symbolism, O’Connor was able to convey the characters’ unique features with their names, connection to parents with Hulga’s wooden leg. The hollow Bible was an allegory for the insincerity of some religious workers.
