Ethical dilemmas in the health care field are challenging to discuss due to the medical procedures’ long-term effect on people’s wellbeing. This paper investigates the case of a retained foreign object (RFO) and the decision-making process of the director of clinical services. The dilemma arises from the hospitals’ inadequate system for reporting and dealing with errors such as RFOs.
