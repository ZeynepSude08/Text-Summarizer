whether a company should produce any product for which there is a demand can be seen as equally relevant to organizational management and business ethics.
additionally, the ethical and moral sides of business production, and the firms corporate social responsibility might be at stake if one was to agree with the titular claim.
in conclusion, the titular claim is thereby denied, since the mere existence of demand is insufficient for a company to make decisions regarding the production of a new set of goods.
market demand is arguably one of the most important factors that stakeholders consider when initiating a new production activity.
