through the poem primer for blacks , gwendolyn brooks encourages black people to embrace blackness and demystifies the analogy of white supremacy.
brooks dispels that it is impossible to achieve equality with the inferior perception and lack of acceptance among the black people.
brooks, through the poem prime for blacks, challenges and encourages the black people to embrace who they are, redefine how they want to be perceived, and stop believing the inferiority propaganda constructed in society, for them to be truly free and attain their full potential.
