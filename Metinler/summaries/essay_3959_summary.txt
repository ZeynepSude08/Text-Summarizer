the theater play, the tragedy of richard ii, is directed by charles bouchard from the brussels shakespeare society.
the story of the overthrow of the king of england richard ii by his cousin henry bolingbroke, the future king henry iv, unfolds among monochrome ascetic scenery to the sound of music bouchard, 2013.
therefore, with a clarity that leaves no room for ambiguity, richard ii poses the question of whether the inheritance law and the socalled divine right of the king are unshakable.
richard ii is presented in the play as the bearer of the feudal principle of the divine origin of royal power.
