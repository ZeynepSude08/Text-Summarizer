in langston hughes poem i, too, the setting and mood shape the story, portraying the life of an ordinary home in the early 20th century in a rebellious and confident mood.
added to this is a reference to whitmans i hear america singing poem that appears in the first and last lines of the poem.
therefore, hughes 2004 notes this separately in his poem that he too is part of america.
the three components of a poem  setting, mood, and story expressed through action  cannot exist without each other.
