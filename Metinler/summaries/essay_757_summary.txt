the myth of platos cave is a wellknown allegory that is considered one of the critical concepts of objective idealism.
platos allegory of the cave is a timeless characterization of the distinction between appearances and reality, prompting humanity to step out of the ignorance into the glow of sunlight.
at the same time, the former prisoners who are allowed to wander in the cave hold the most accurate view of reality possible in the shifting world of perception and experience bonazzi, 2020.
the essence of this concept is what plato tried to refute in his allegory, claiming that the world outside the cave is actual existence, filled with unchanging and ideal objects of cognition.
