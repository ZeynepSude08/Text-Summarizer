this essay argues that worker vaccination should not be mandatory because it leads to unjust firings, makes information on employees health and beliefs less private, and does not consider natural immunity and its effects.
when vaccination is mandatory, workers have to report private information related to their personal life and health issues.
to start with, vaccination records are treated as confidential information, but employees have to make it open for companies to avoid being fired american academy of family physicians.
one counterargument is that natural immunity might weaken faster than after vaccination magarakis and kelen.
finally, mandatory vaccination for workers is negative because it causes mass firings against peoples will, ignores essential immunity, and makes workers report their private information.
