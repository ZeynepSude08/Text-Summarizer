wellbeing hedonic and eudaimonic philosophies essay
while hedonism suggests that wellbeing should be measured by the amount of pleasure one experiences, eudaimonism argues that it depends on happiness that people get from helping others, as well as fulfilling ones true potential.
many researchers have presented evidence that wellbeing is a complex concept which is most likely to combine both hedonic and eudaimonic conceptions of happiness.
having originally presented the hypothesis on that, huta and ryan 2009 conducted an experiment, in which people had to introduce hedonic and eudaimonic activities to their lives.
