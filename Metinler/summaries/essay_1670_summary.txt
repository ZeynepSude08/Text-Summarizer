spoken word poetry as a form of a literary movement essay
spoken word poetry is defined solely by how it is delivered orally.
writing poetry in the spoken word style has its own set of rules rhyme, rhythm beats and breaks, diction, and parallel sentence structure are all examples of how to incorporate these elements into a poem.
there must be courage in a spoken word poem to communicate ones thoughts and feelings to the world.
spoken word poetry allows one to be anybody they wish to be.
in this sense, spoken word poetry allows young people to become active agents in their rehabilitation.
we can make spoken word poetry whatever we want it to be.
