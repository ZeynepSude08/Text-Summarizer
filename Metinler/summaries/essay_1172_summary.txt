in his article for the explicator, stephen slimp argues that at the end of where are you going, where have you been?, connie has achieved a depth of spirit through her encounter with arnold and that her screaming, born of her encounter with evil, results in her trying to establish a proper relationship with another human beingin this case, her mother p.
though i agree with slimp that connie desperately wants her mother as she comes to understand the depth of evil arnold represents, the third and sixth paragraphs of the story suggest that the reason connie is in such a precarious position to begin with and leaves with arnold in the end is that nothing in her social world has provided her with effective weapons or support against him.
critical analysis of these two paragraphs reveals that connie succumbs to arnold and leaves with him at the end of the story because of her social inexperience and her familys emotional neglect.
