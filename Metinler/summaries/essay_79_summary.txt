anthrax can be commonly found in soil normally and can infect domestic and wild animals such as livestock, sheep, and goats figueiredo, 2019.
people can become infected with anthrax by managing diseased animal protein or inhaling anthrax spores from animal foods.
people can also become infected with anthrax by eating raw meat from infected animals or being subjected to pathogens by bioterrorists.
anthrax can make individuals sick if they come into the consumption of contaminated food or infected animal protein.
bacillus anthracis, a sporeforming bacterial infection that can sustain in harsh environments for long periods, causes anthrax.
individuals can lower their risk of anthrax infection by being immunized.
